Rules and Bylaws

  1. All grading, landscaping, and improvements of any kind, and all care on plots shall be done, and all trees and shrubs and herbage of any kind shall be planted, trimmed, cut or removed and all openings and closings of plots, and all interments, disinterment and removals shall be made only by the TCCA unless by special permit from the TCCA in writing.
  2. The Trinity Community Cemetery Association (TCCA) reserves the right to remove all floral designs, flowers, weeds, trees, shrubs, and herbage of any kind, from the cemetery as soon as, in judgment of the TCCA, they become unsightly, dangerous, detrimental, or diseased, or when they do not conform to the standards maintained by the rest of the cemeteries.
  3. The TCCA shall not be held liable for floral pieces, baskets, or frames in which or to which such floral pieces are attached, beyond the acceptance of such floral pieces for funeral servce held in cemetery They shall not be liable for lost, misplaced, or broken flower vases.
  4. The TCCA shall not be responsible for frozen plants or herbage of any kind, or for plantings damaged by the elements, thieves, vandals, or by causes beyond its control.
  5. The TCCA reserves the right to prevent the removal of any flowers, floral designs, trees, shrubs, or plants, or herbage, of any kind unless the TCCA gives written consent.
  6. The following items are strictly prohibitted, and if so placed, the TCCA reserves the right to remove:
    a.) The placing of boxes, shells, toys, metal designs, ornaments, chairs.
    b.) Glass, wood, or iron cases and similar articles.
    c.) Shrubs on lots without monuments.
    d.) Marble chips around monuments or markers.
    e.) Window boxes, pots, glass jars, etc., except for one week before and after Memorial Day.
  7. All fittings, adornments, urns, inscriptions, and arrangements of crypts or niches shall be, and are hereby declared to be, subject to the approval and control of, and acceptance or rejection by the TCCA.
  8. As a special mark of respect for those that have served our Country, the American flag shall be displayed only on the grave of those persons who have honorably served in the armed forces of the United States of America.
  9. Artificial flowers must be removed and renewed within one (1) month and fresh flowers must be removed and renewed within one (1) week.

Our Story

---

Trinity Community Cemetery Association (TCCA) was established in 1963. The purpose of TCCA is to provide an opportunity for persons who have loved ones buried in the Trinity Community Cemetery to: preserve the memory of our loved ones, make contributions for perpetual care or/ up-keep of the grounds and surroundings and effectively operate the organization.
Membership is open to any willing and interested families and/or family members in Trinity and vicinity who have people buried in the Trinity Cemetery or who plans to bury people in the Trinity Community Cemetery.

The TCCA was originally organized by Trinity community leaders: Rev. A.E. Baines, Mr. O.T. Ligon, Mrs. Hattie M. Baker and Mr. Loyd Rawls who came together with the support of Trinity's 13 black churches to form the formerly known Trinity Cemetery Committee. The original objectives of the committee was to: buy land, fence in all (3) cemeteries, purchase a flag pole, and build a small maintenance house. The Trinity Cemetery Committee members of 1963 were: Rev. A. E. Baines- Supervisor, Mr. C.L. Jones- President, Mr. Willie Tillis- Vice President, Mr. Loyd Rawls- Superintendent, Mr. O.T. Ligons- Maintenance Manager, Mr. Leon Butler- Assistant Maintenance Manager, Rev. James Walker- Caretaker, Mrs. Penola Cunningham- Secretary, Mrs. Deleta Curtis- Treasurer, and Mrs. Hattie M. Baker- Publicity Chairman. The Original 13 Black Churches were: Lone Star M.B.C, Bowen Pilgrim M.B.C, Campbell Memorial C.O.G.I.C, Harris Chapel C.M.E. Church, Trinity Chapel A.M.E. Church, Church of God in Christ, Mt. Sinai M. B. Church, Church of Christ, Mt. Sinai Primitive B. Church, Westside Church of God in Christ, Clegg Chapel M.B. Church, Mary Chapel M.B. Church, and Bible Way Baptist Church.
From 1942-1954, a plot at the Trinity Community Cemetery cost $2. Between 1954-1969, a plot cost $4. From 1969-1981, a plot cost $10 and January 1981, plot costs increased to $25. To view current plot prices, visit here.
